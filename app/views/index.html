Incident Management & Resilience Local Outcome Plan 2020-2025

This Local Outcome Plan sets out how the Incident Management & Resilience (IM&R) team will deliver the strategic goals set out in the EA action plan (EA2025). It outlines our aspirations and objectives for the next 5 years.

Foreword

Incident Management & Resilience Team

IM&R are in the Local Operations directorate. We set the vision, values and standards of the Environment Agency's incident management service.

We deliver excellent technical leadership alongside national incident response.

Who we are

Preparedness and Recovery

Helping society plan, prepare and recovery quickly.

  • Evolve and continually improve our response arrangements including our 9-5 tactical first response and form a core of dedicated capable responders.

  • Improve the capability and confidence of our incident responders embedding the Incident Management Centre for Excellence.

  • Develop relationships with partners to ensure we collaborate and provide a resilient national response service.

  • Develop our recovery framework.

Readiness and Response

Ensuring we are ready to respond to any incident to th required standards using the plans, technology and people across IM&R.

  • Run the National Incident room.

  • Improve our incident air quality monitoring capability and capacity to enable a more effective response.

  • Create a focus for forecasting leadership within IM&R to enable a transition to a national forecasting service.

Evidence and Risk

Providing analysis and advice to reduce the impacts of hazards and improve resilience.

  • Provision of expert modelling, forecasting and technical services to ensure our understanding of environmental, flood and coastal change risks.

    >

  • Prepare accurate community flood forecasts, and build and maintain flood assets to better protect people, communities, the environment and the economy.

    >

  • Delivery of improvements to cope with a changing climate including the Flood Hydrology Roadmap, improved flood data quality and increased flood warning coverage using innovative approaches.

Strategy, Service and Investment

Setting the strategic direction, securing investment and overseeing the delivery of one incident management service.

  • Coordinate and track the year one priorities for the IM Strategy.

  • Implement the IM Service Approach for Incident Management working with delivery teams across the business.

  • IM has its own allocation with a clear understanding of how we use this to deliver our service and the value this brings by tying it close to effective and streamlined business planning.

  • Revise IM assurance metrics with a clear line of sight between service standards, KPIs and corporate scorecard measures so we incentivise the behaviours and changes we want to see.

Digital Services

Developing easy to use, reliable and available systems.

  • Management and maintenance of essential digital services and systems.

  • Run and embed digital improvement projects including IR&T, XWS, IMFS, Logistics and Resources Planning Tool (MAT/ERT).

  • Horizon scan considering and mitigating impacts of Corporate IT changes on IM end to end services including Windows 10, Zscaler, O365, New IT or laptops.
